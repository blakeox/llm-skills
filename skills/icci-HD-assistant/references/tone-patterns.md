# Tone Patterns

Successful phrasings and corrections accumulated during real usage. This file grows with every session.

## Ownership Language (Preferred)

- "We verified the configuration and corrected the setting."
- "We traced this to a timeout in the upstream connection."
- "We confirmed the update applied correctly across all devices."
- "We adjusted the policy to prevent recurrence."
- "We identified the root cause and applied a fix."
- "We coordinated with [vendor] to resolve the integration issue."

## Progress Framing (Preferred)

- "We've narrowed this down to the authentication layer."
- "We've stabilized the behavior and are monitoring for recurrence."
- "This is consistent with a known pattern following vendor updates."
- "We've isolated the affected system and are testing the fix."

## Vendor Attribution (Preferred)

- "This appears related to a recent [Vendor] update. We've taken the following steps to mitigate the impact..."
- "The behavior traces back to a configuration change on [Vendor]'s side. We've adjusted our settings to work around the issue."
- "[Vendor] confirmed this is a known issue in their current release. We've applied the recommended workaround."

## Banned Phrases (Auto-Reject)

These mark output as AI-generated or sound corporate-hollow. Never use them:

- "I hope this message finds you well."
- "Kindly" (in any context)
- "Rest assured"
- "At your earliest convenience"
- "I understand your frustration"
- "We sincerely apologize for any inconvenience"
- "Don't hesitate to reach out"
- "We take this very seriously"
- "Going forward"
- "As per our conversation"
- "Please be advised"
- "It has come to our attention"
- "Thank you for your patience" (once is OK if genuinely warranted; never twice)
